About Me

Hi! I'm Pat Hayes. I grew up in Cleveland and went to the University of Dayton where I got a degree in applied mathematical economics. I recently finished the full-stack full-time web development code immersion camp at UNCC, which was pretty intense.

I've played some odd sports in my time including Gaelic football, hurling, australian rules football and rugby. The first two trace back to my Irish heritage and my involvement in the Cleveland Irish community. Although there is more to know about me this was a quick start!

Education

University of Dayton

The University of Dayton is where I spent four years achieving my undergrad. I started off in the engineering program moving from Electrical Engineering to eventually Applied Mathematical Economics. This bachelor of science gave me upper level core math skills as well as an economic basis where I could apply such skills. Out of all the classes I took, I probably had the most fun in 'Probability and Statistics' as well as Econometrics and using regression analysis.

Cleveland State University

During the summer breaks from Dayton, I also took courses closer to home at Cleveland State University where my dad worked until retirement. I was able to take undergraduate finance courses and had various roles in the student IS&T department. I was network technician, lab monitor, and student hardware/software technician. The first and last of the roles involved resolving tickets across the campus for things like: installing/hooking up network cables, finding and removing malware, diagnosing hardware failure, ordering replacement parts, installing replacement hardwawre, configing software for new users. All of these things on the job were a great learning experience for me.

Udemy

After all these college days were over, I began looking at online coding courses through Udemy. I would work on these courses in my spare time. It was nice in that compared to most college courses it was far cheaper, unfortunately this seemed a little slow and too segmented for me making them take a while to finish...

University of North Carolina at Charlotte

So more recently I took a code immersion camp for full stack web development at UNCC. I took this course full time, leaving Cleveland and moving to South of Charlotte to do so. The program was intense, not everyone made it, but I'm quite proud of those who did finish with me. Doing this everyday and going over such a large breadth of material really pushed me to learn quickly. It had plenty of deadlines to manage as well as context switching as we went through many different languages, libraries, and technologies. One of the biggest things it gave me was ability to look into my own problems and teach myself new things to overcome current problems. Quite a few of the technologies used in my projects had to be self-taught. Through all of this, I've added quite a number of skills to my skill set.
